This document introduces **Busan**, the second largest city in South Korea.  
It is designed for travelers and learners who want to explore Korean culture and cities.

## About Busan

Busan is a vibrant coastal city known for its **beaches, mountains, and delicious food**.  
It is located in the southeastern part of South Korea.

## Famous Places

- **Haeundae Beach (해운대 해수욕장)**: The most famous beach in Busan. Great for swimming, sunbathing, and festivals.
- **Gwangalli Beach (광안리 해수욕장)**: Known for its **night view of Gwangan Bridge** and lively cafes.
- **Gamcheon Culture Village (감천문화마을)**: A colorful village with art murals, cute streets, and galleries.
- **Jagalchi Fish Market (자갈치 시장)**: Korea’s largest seafood market, offering fresh fish and local delicacies.
- **Beomeosa Temple (범어사)**: A beautiful Buddhist temple located in the mountains, perfect for cultural experiences.

## Food to Try

- **Ssiat Hotteok (씨앗호떡)**: Sweet Korean pancake filled with seeds and sugar.
- **Milmyeon (밀면)**: Cold wheat noodles, a local specialty of Busan.
- **Hoe (회)**: Fresh raw fish, usually served at Jagalchi Market.

> Tip: Take the Busan Metro to explore the city easily and try street food near the beaches.
