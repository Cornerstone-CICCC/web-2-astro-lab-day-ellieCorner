This document introduces **Seoul**, the capital and largest city of South Korea.  
It is designed for travelers and learners who want to explore Korean culture, history, and city life.

## About Seoul

Seoul is a dynamic city where **modern skyscrapers meet ancient palaces**.  
It is located in the northwest part of South Korea and is home to over 10 million people.

## Famous Places

- **Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁)**: The main royal palace of the Joseon Dynasty, offering a glimpse into Korea’s history.
- **Bukchon Hanok Village (북촌한옥마을)**: A traditional village with hanok houses and narrow streets.
- **N Seoul Tower (N서울타워)**: A popular observation tower providing panoramic views of the city.
- **Myeongdong (명동)**: A bustling shopping district full of fashion, cosmetics, and street food.
- **Dongdaemun Design Plaza (동대문디자인플라자)**: A modern cultural hub with exhibitions, shops, and architecture.

## Food to Try

- **Bibimbap (비빔밥)**: Mixed rice with vegetables, meat, and chili paste.
- **Tteokbokki (떡볶이)**: Spicy rice cakes, a popular street food.
- **Samgyeopsal (삼겹살)**: Grilled pork belly, often enjoyed with friends.

> Tip: Use the Seoul Metro to move around quickly and experience different neighborhoods in one day.
